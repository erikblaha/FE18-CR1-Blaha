We present: The best recipe in the month

These Homemade Doughnuts are:

  • Soft & Sweet
  • Perfect for sharing and caring
  • Ready in under 2 hours!
  • My all-time favorites

Ingredients

  • 1¼ cups milk
  • 2¼ teaspoons (one package) active dry yeast
  • 2 eggs
  • 8 tablespoons (1 stick) butter, melted and cooled
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4¼ cups all-purpose flour, plus more for rolling out the dough
  • 2 quarts neutral oil, for frying, plus more for the bowl.

Preparations and directions

  1. Heat the milk until it is warm but not hot, about 90 degrees. In a large bowl, combine it with the yeast. Stir lightly, and let sit until the mixture is foamy, about 5 minutes.
  2. Mix the eggs, butter, sugar and salt into the yeast mixture. Add half of the flour (2 cups plus 2 tablespoons), and mix until combined. Transfer the dough to a large bowl, and cover. Let rise at room temperature until it doubles in size, about 1 hour.
  3. Knead any scraps together, being careful not to overwork the dough. Cut out the doughnuts with a doughnut cutter or concentric cookie cutters or a drinking glass. If you're making filled doughnuts, don't cut out the middle; reserve the doughnut holes.
  4. Put the doughnuts on two floured baking sheets so that there is plenty of room between each one. Cover with a kitchen towel, and let rise in a warm place until they are slightly puffed up and delicate, about 45 minutes. If your kitchen isn’t warm, heat the oven to 200 at the beginning of this step, then turn off the heat, put the baking sheets in the oven and leave the door ajar.
  5. About 15 minutes before the doughnuts are done rising, put the oil in a heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat, and heat it to 375. Meanwhile, line cooling racks, baking sheets or plates with paper towels.
  6. Use a metal spatula to pick up the delicate doughnuts and slide them into the oil. When the bottoms are deep golden, after 45 seconds to a minute, use a slotted spoon to flip them. Glaze or fill as follows, and serve as soon as possible.
